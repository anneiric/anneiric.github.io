✧ the straight mind

monique wittig

feminist essay on heterormativity, the term "woman" was made by patriarchy to oppress and thus lesbians aren't women, as they go against that binary.

"if we, as lesbians and gay men, continue to speak of ourselfves and to conceieve of ourselves as women and as men, we are instrumental in maintaining heterosexuality"

"once the class "men" disappears, "women" as a class will disappear as well, for there are no slaves without masters."

✧ hurricane season

fernanda melchor

murder of a witch in mexico, maybe queer?? corruption, poverty and misogyny. spiralling prose with no paragraphs.

"grandma hit yesenia where it hurt most, dying right there, trembling with hate in the arms of her eldest granddaughter."

"they say she never really died, because witches don’t go without a fight."

"respect the dead silence of that house, the pain of the miserable souls who once lived there. that’s what the women in town say: there is no treasure in there, no gold or silver or diamonds or anything more than a searing pain that refuses to go away."

"there was something evil and terrible inside her for wanting that contact, that crude embrace, and for wanting it to last forever."

✧ the dangers of smoking in bed

mariana enriquez

a collection of eerie short stories: a ghost of a baby, jealousy at friend's boyfriend and a hidden lake with dogs and lies, a homeless elderly man cursing a neighbourhood, josefina having so many phobias and taking so many medicine and going to the witch, ghost kids in stinking barcelona, ghost and suicidal woman trading places, hearts and saws, music fans eating raw dead meat, nico and his photography, missing kids coming back and dead vanadis is alive, ouija boards.

"i made her run after me on her bare little feet that, rotten as they were, left her little white bones in view."

"coca ate her cat, and then she killed herself."

"if it was slow, she kept her hand against her chest to be sure her heart didn’t stop."

"josefina stood up with what was left of the air in her lungs, with the new strength that fortified her legs. it wasn’t going to last long, she was sure, but please, let it be enough, enough to run to the well and throw myself into the rainwater, and please let it be bottomless so i can drown there with the photo and the betrayal."

"she sank into the bathtub and went back over the cuts, so the blood would float around her and turn the water red. it was beautiful. she put her head underwater and opened her eyes to an ocean of reddish swirls."

"a moribund person walking, a person whose mind didn't register the death of the body."

✧ dear life

alice munro

short stories of childhood trauma, broken wedding love, military apathy, death and kidnappings.

"isabel was finally gone. they said “gone,” as if she had got up and left. when someone had checked her about an hour ago, she had been the same as ever, and now she was gone."

"we were just leaving.” we. he has said we. for a moment i cling to that word. then i think it’s the last time. the last time i’ll be included in his we."

"there’s always one morning when you realize that the birds have all gone."
