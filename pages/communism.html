C O M M U N I S M

the patriarchal and heterosexual organisations in society have been passively instrumental in forming and maintaining capitalism and its repressive nature. communism thus strives for the abolition of private property and simultaneously the oppressive structure of society, thereby culminating in the end of the exploitation of class. communism, as a form of ideology, generally inculcates a utopian idea, in which once the class “bourgeoisie” disappears, the “proletariat”, as a class will disappear as well, for there are no slaves without masters. however, such idea is often exclusive of race, gender and sexuality, as it calls into question some of the most basic assumptions about our existence, i.e., that class is the only determinant of power relationships. the standardised view of capitalism is perpetuated by the same forces that perpetuate colonialism, patriarchy, white supremacy and gender. it is thus necessary to form any communism-related ideology in an attempt to abolish any and all inequalities ubiquitous in societal, power dynamics and deconstruct these ideas. resources on communism inclusive of factors other than class can be found here:
