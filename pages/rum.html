R U M

i really wanted to kiss you that day. the fumes of your copper coated cigarette were rising up out of your chest, into the air, as we stared at the swirls deforming the bleeding sunset. if i shut my eyes hard enough, i could taste your lips, red, with an unforgiving sweetness, like a thousand bursting berries on virgin taste buds.

as your teeth fumbled with the lighter, i told you about marx and monique wittig. i made a joke about seizing the means of production and you grabbed my chin, chuckling.

"look at me"

i looked at you and i looked at you and i looked at you. i looked at your swan neck that gave rise to a soft jaw. i looked at the ocean salt tessellating your cheeks. i looked at your eyes, with emerald, pearled tears of alcoholic insomnia. i looked at the mole beside your eyebrow, bleached by the tesco concealer we bought at 3am, and i saw that you would rip the sky in two for me as hades would for persephone. the gods in the heavens above us rumbled with laugher. laugher because hades ruined persephone - and we too; the two lesbian lovers with an ambrosia and nicotine addiction.

"do you know what the universe tastes like?" you asked me.

"nothing?”

“no, not nothing;" you flicked your eyes at our hands; to the faint shape of a bathtub that we stick and poked and whispered, "rum and raspberries.”

“this has to be made up,” i scoffed.

“it’s not!” you grinned. i loved your smile. “there’s a dust cloud in the centre of the universe that is primarily made of a chemical called ethyl formate. it’s the same chemical that gives flavour to raspberries and scent to rum.”

i told you that i loved you, when i sat on your counter in low spongebob socks and picked at the skin of my banana. you were drunk then. and you probably didn't remember it when you woke up behind the couch with a pink bandage on your knee.

it's ok though. every time i kiss a new girl, i remember your taste. rum and raspberries.
